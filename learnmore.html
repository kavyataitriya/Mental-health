Types of Mental Disorders

1. Anxiety Disorder

Anxiety disorders are characterized by severe fear or anxiety related to certain objects or situations, often leading to avoidance behavior.
Types of Anxiety Disorders :

  1. Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)
  2. Panic Disorder
  3. Phobias (including specific phobias, social phobia, and agoraphobia)
  4. Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)
  5. Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

2. Mood Disorders

Mood disorders, also known as affective or depressive disorders, are characterized by significant changes in mood. These changes typically involve episodes of either mania, characterized by heightened energy and euphoria, or depression, marked by persistent feelings of sadness or loss of interest in previously enjoyed activities (anhedonia).

  1. Major Depression
  2. Bipolar Disorder
  3. Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD)

3. Schizophrenia Disorders

The term “schizophrenia” often refers to a spectrum of disorders characterized by features of psychosis and other severe symptoms. These are highly complex conditions.
